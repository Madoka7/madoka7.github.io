A letter to Tian

2017-04-30 • Categories: Experience

My visa application has been approved this morning. Here is the process I experienced.

Simplified version:
OV:
Give me your passport and i20 plz.
Me:
Here you are.
OV:
Why URI?
Me:
Because we have cooperated before and prof. Wang adviced me to study with prof. Li.
OV:
What is your current major, why EE?
Me:
Network Engineering, close to ee.
OV:
Oh, PhD hum. Your CV plz.
Me:
Hya
OV:
Your Prof’s CV plz.
Me:
Hya
OV:
(Typing…) Your visa has been approved.(Kept my passport and gave me a pink paper.)
Me:
Thx sir.

My appointed time was 8: 30 am, so I got up at 7 and went to wait at 8.
At 8:17, an officer noticed candidate of 8:30 are allowed to enter the council.
We queued at Window 1 and the officer asked for DS160 and passport. She adhered an sticker on my passport.
Then I was waiting after an queue, untill another officer informed me to enter Window 2 (Let’s call it room 2). Then there was a security check. Then queue. A Chinese officer viewed my passport and I 20 then let me queue to talk to OV.
Then review the simplified version.

BTW, things that are not allowed to take with:
Keys
Coins
Phones
Unopened Envelope (Exclude transparent plastic)

Sincerely,
Xiangqi
